About

I am Vice Director of the IDA Lab and lead the Smart Analytics & Reinforcement Learning (SARL) team at PLUS University in Salzburg. My research spans from theoretical foundations to real-world applications in industry. I also serve as a Visiting Scientist at CERN, where I apply AI to the world's largest particle accelerators.

My work focuses on developing robust, modern AI systems that make good decisions under uncertainty. I believe that human and artificial intelligence are complementary and that their combination is key to solving our current global challenges.

Research Interests: Sample-efficient reinforcement learning, safe RL, model-based methods, meta-learning, applications in accelerator physics, robotics, healthcare, and industrial control systems.

Background

My team focuses on various flavors of reinforcement learning, multivariate time series and analytics, Bayesian methods, control theory, and physics. Before joining PLUS, I worked at CERN in Geneva for several years, where I completed my PhD on electromagnetic field effects in particle accelerators (selected for a prestigious scholarship by the Austrian PhD program).
